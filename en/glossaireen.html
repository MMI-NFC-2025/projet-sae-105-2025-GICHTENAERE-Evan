GLOSSARY

Nobel Prize: The Nobel Prize is an international award created in 1901, which honors each year individuals who have made a major contribution to humanity in the fields of peace, literature, medicine, chemistry, physics, and economics.

→ Read more

Isotope: Atoms of the same chemical element that have the same number of protons but a different number of neutrons. This means they share the same chemical properties but have different masses.

→ Read more

Cobalt-60 is a radioactive isotope of cobalt mainly used in medicine for radiotherapy and in industry for sterilization. It emits powerful gamma rays.

→ Read more

Radioactivity is the ability of certain unstable atomic nuclei to transform into other nuclei by emitting radiation (alpha, beta, or gamma).

→ Read more

Matilda Effect: An injustice in which the discoveries of women scientists are ignored or attributed to men.
